Notes on seminar on MMT

Seminar on MMT

Firstly we discussed what is money in MMT. A very important idea about MMT is that money is created by the government as a credit in tax. The fiat currency is used only because people need to pay the tax with the currency.

Then we start to examine a very popular point in MMT, which is government can spend as much as they want. The reason they spend the money is because the action will produce positive effect to the economy. Some comments on what if the government don’t drain out the liquidity it creates were made, but the topic is focused on federal funds rate(primary rate). As I point out, it doesn’t matter how interest rate or other intermediate factor changes. The only thing that is important is unemployment rate(in other word total output) and inflation rate(in other word living standard of people).

How government spending affects economy

After that we turned to a very interest discussion on how will government spending affect the economy. As we can see, there are 3 type of ways the government can do their spending:

  • give money to the rich

    • What will happen?
      • Giving money to the rich will increase saving(rich people’s mpc is very low).
      • S increase will cause an increase in either capital outflow or increase in investment(normally both).
    • When is it good?
      • When the economy faces a constrain to investment. Which is due to a limit in capital resource, the economy’s actual investment is lower than the desired investment.
      • In this condition, increasing investment will inject capital to investment opportunities that is effective for the economy which will increase the total output and also benefits the poor.
    • When is it bad?
      • When the economy have no constrain to invest. Then the injection of capital will either flow outside of the country or cause a decrease in consumption. Since GDP = C+I+NX, if I increases and others remain the same, then consumption will decrease.

  • give money to the poor

    • What will happen?

      • Giving money to the poor will increase consumption(poor people’s mpc is very high).

    • When is it good?

      • When there is enough supply, but the consumption power is too low.
      • Example: US in great recession, China 2019
      • An increase in consumption will actually also stimulate the investment. Hence the total economy will be better off with higher output.
    • When is it bad?
      • In a full capacity economy, this will only cause inflation and nothing else.

  • Build infrastructure

    • When is it good?
      • When there are a lot of investment opportunities that have great positive externalities. So only the government can invest in those projects
      • The whole output of the economy will increase
    • When is it bad?
      • When the infrastructure in economy is very developed. Further investment are all unproductive investment.

This discussion had tell us what the government should and should not do in different economies. And the MMT give us a strong support on stating that the government can spend what ever they want(the maximum is the potential output of the economy) while not to worry about debt and inflation since debt doesn’t matter and inflation can be easily controlled.

What should a government do?

Also through this analysis, we can see that is a perfect job the a government can do in the economy. As we look at each condition, base on our knowledge on the decrease of marginal production, we can see that once we did the good policy to a level, the economy situation will make the policy a bad one(This plus political can explain a lot of terrible fiscal policy). So the optimal government spending is to let economy go to the situation that all additional spending will create a negative effect. If we list them out, they are:

  • let economy don’t have investment constrain
  • let the economy run at full capacity
  • let the economy have enough infrastructure, but not too much

This is very difficult for a government to make decision on when to stop and when to start. The government is capable of fixing inflation rate, interest rate, but this kind of information will need too much information and computation power that current society can’t provide.

Automatic Stabilizer and Policy

Again, I want us to look back at MMT theory. The key policy suggestion from MMT theory in my understanding is not how and where the government should spend and get the money back. Instead the core of MMT’s policy suggestion is the automatic stabilizer. Two good examples are:

  • Progressive tax
    • Since tax is affecting rich more than the poor, investment is more effected by tax
    • When the economy goes well, the government will drain more liquidity out the economy and slow down the investment
    • When the economy goes bad, the government will take less money from the economy so the economy can do more investment
  • Guarantee job program
    • Since the program is affecting the poor, consumption is more effected
    • When the economy is going well, private firms can hire more people and the government will hire less. So less money is injected and the consumer will decrease
    • When the economy is going bad, more workers go to the government jobs and force the government to inject more money to the poor and consumer will increase

With the automatic stabilizer, the government don’t have to do much to improve the economy. Or even better, the government can use the stabilizer as a signal to make their decisions since the change in the cost of those stabilizers does have true economic meanings.

Problem with Europe

MMT is mainly used in country will a sovereign currency, the country can print the currency and borrow with the currency. Most of the developed country like USA, Britain, Australia, Japan follows this requirement. But since the creation of Euro, all of the Europe Union countries do not fit the condition.

This means that countries in EU can not do what is optimal for its country as we discussed above. Especially when the Central Bank of Europe is now even trying to control each countries fiscal policy, this make economic problem that could be solve impossible in Europe. This might be a great explanation to why Europe is suffering ever since EU has formed. Not because they lost their monetary policy freedom, but because they lost their fiscal policy freedom.

This makes each country of Europe just like a state in US. They don’t have their own monetary policy and the debt to GDP ratio can only be very low. In history, there are many states in US that go bankrupt, just like Europe nowadays. So a new way to study Europe countries might be looking at how states in US, province in China or other similar units that is lower than the level of a country operates.
